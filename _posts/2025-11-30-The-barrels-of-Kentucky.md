It's a rather sunny Saturday morning. The sun blares through the window's glass panes. It feels a little too late to be in bed. I’ve been like this for two hours now—groggy eyes and an aching body. I can’t seem to get up. Woolly sleeps right next to me on the bed; he seems to be resting fine, even with the sun blasting his white fur with heat. It’s been two weeks since father’s passing. I miss hearing the tractors running wild early in the morning.
